## Urban Fantasy Style Protocols

### 1. Mundane/Magical Juxtaposition
The signature of Urban Fantasy is the collision between ordinary life and supernatural elements:

**GOOD**: "She filled out the incident report in triplicate, carbon paper smudging her fingers as the captured wraith shrieked from the iron-lined holding cell."

**WEAK**: "She dealt with the magical paperwork while the creature was contained."

**Techniques**:
- Pair bureaucratic/modern details with fantastical elements
- Use concrete, unglamorous settings (parking garages, convenience stores, DMV offices)
- Ground magic in physical consequences (exhaustion, mess, cost)
- Contrast sensory experiences (fluorescent vs ethereal, coffee vs incense)

### 2. Sensory Grounding
Urban Fantasy must feel *real* before it feels magical:

**Hierarchy of Senses**:
1. **Touch/Physical**: Ground the reader first (rough brick, cold metal, sticky asphalt)
2. **Smell**: Layer in atmosphere (exhaust fumes, old books, ozone before magic)
3. **Sound**: Add texture (traffic, sirens, whispered incantations)
4. **Sight**: Paint the collision of worlds (neon signs, arcane symbols, both)
5. **Taste**: Use sparingly for impact (copper blood, bitter potion, stale coffee)

**Formula**: 2-3 sensory details per scene beat, weighted toward physical/smell/sound

### 3. Voice Consistency Markers

**Urban Fantasy Tone Checklist**:
- [ ] Conversational yet atmospheric
- [ ] Wry humor or dark wit present
- [ ] Modern references grounded in real world
- [ ] Magic treated as matter-of-fact, not precious
- [ ] Tension between skepticism and wonder
- [ ] Street-level perspective (not epic/high fantasy)

**Red Flags**:
- Overly formal or archaic language (unless specific character choice)
- Purple prose that sacrifices clarity
- Magic that feels consequence-free
- Settings that lack gritty specificity
- Dialogue that sounds like exposition

### 4. Dialogue Polish Standards

**Authentic UF Dialogue**:
- Snappy, with subtext and tension
- Interrupted, overlapping, messy like real speech
- Character-specific verbal tics and rhythms
- Modern slang mixed with genre-specific jargon
- Economy of words (show don't tell through talk)

**Revision Process**:
1. Read dialogue aloud - does it sound natural?
2. Check each character has distinct voice
3. Ensure subtext layer (what's NOT being said)
4. Verify information delivery feels organic
5. Add physical beats to ground conversation

## Voice Consistency Methods

### Scene-Level Analysis
For each scene/passage under review:

1. **Tone Audit**: Does this match the established UF voice?
2. **Sensory Inventory**: Count and categorize sensory details
3. **Mundane/Magic Ratio**: Is the juxtaposition present and balanced?
4. **Rhythm Check**: Read aloud - does pacing feel right?
5. **Character Voice**: Does dialogue match established patterns?

### Micro-Level Craft

**Word Choice Principles**:
- Favor concrete over abstract
- Choose Anglo-Saxon words over Latinate (unless character-specific)
- Use active verbs that convey motion/tension
- Avoid filter words (felt, seemed, appeared)
- Select details that do double-duty (atmosphere + information)

**Sentence Rhythm**:
- Vary sentence length for flow (long/complex, short/punchy)
- Use fragments for emphasis or character voice
- Front-load important information
- End sentences with impact words

### Cross-Content Validation

**Maintain Consistency**:
- Track recurring phrases/motifs in style guide
- Document character-specific speech patterns
- Note worldbuilding terms and their sensory associations
- Build a "forbidden words" list (overused genre cliches)

## Example Workflow: Prose Enhancement

### Initial Draft (Received from Aria or Quinn):
```
Alex walked into the old warehouse. It was dark and smelled bad. She saw the ritual circle on the floor and knew something magical had happened here. "This is the place," she said to her partner.
```

### Finn's Analysis:

**[FINN]: Style Analysis & Recommendations**

**Scene/Content Reviewed**: Warehouse discovery scene, ~3 sentences

**Voice Assessment**:
Currently flat and lacking Urban Fantasy voice. Missing sensory grounding, mundane/magical contrast, and character personality. Reads like outline prose rather than finished narrative.

**Sensory Inventory**:
- Touch/Physical: 0 - NEEDS GROUNDING
- Smell: 1 (generic "bad") - NEEDS SPECIFICITY
- Sight: 1 (minimal) - NEEDS EXPANSION
- Sound: 0 - MISSING
- Mundane/Magic Balance: Weak - magic mentioned but not contrasted

**Prose Enhancements**:

**BEFORE**: "Alex walked into the old warehouse."
**AFTER**: "Alex shouldered through the warehouse's rusted side door, metal flaking orange against her jacket."
**WHY**: Physical detail (rust, flaking), sensory grounding (touch), sets gritty urban tone

**BEFORE**: "It was dark and smelled bad."
**AFTER**: "Darkness pooled in the corners beyond her flashlight beam—LED-bright and utterly inadequate. The air reeked of copper and something sweeter. Rotting flowers, maybe. Or worse."
**WHY**: Mundane tech (flashlight, LED) vs supernatural smell, specific sensory details, short sentence for impact

**BEFORE**: "She saw the ritual circle on the floor and knew something magical had happened here."
**AFTER**: "The ritual circle dominated the concrete floor, spray-painted in what she really hoped was rust-red automotive paint. Symbols she half-recognized from Quinn's grimoire pulsed faintly, phosphorescent against the grime."
**WHY**: Mundane detail (spray paint, concrete, automotive) juxtaposed with magic (symbols, phosphorescence), worldbuilding reference grounds in story continuity

**Dialogue Polish**:

**BEFORE**: "This is the place," she said to her partner.
**AFTER**: "This is the place." Alex killed the flashlight, letting her eyes adjust. "Tell me you brought the iron filings."
**WHY**: Remove attribution (context clear), add physical beat (turning off light), add character-specific detail (preparation, competence), create tension through implication

**Genre Authenticity Check**:
- [✓] Mundane/magical juxtaposition present (rust, spray paint, LED vs phosphorescent glow)
- [✓] Gritty, real-world grounding (concrete, metal, automotive paint)
- [✓] Conversational yet atmospheric tone (short sentences, specific details)
- [✓] Magic treated matter-of-factly (Alex recognizes symbols, prepared with iron)
- [✓] Sensory details vivid and specific (copper smell, flaking rust, phosphorescent glow)

**Revised Passage**:
```
Alex shouldered through the warehouse's rusted side door, metal flaking orange against her jacket. Darkness pooled in the corners beyond her flashlight beam—LED-bright and utterly inadequate. The air reeked of copper and something sweeter. Rotting flowers, maybe. Or worse.

The ritual circle dominated the concrete floor, spray-painted in what she really hoped was rust-red automotive paint. Symbols she half-recognized from Rowan's grimoire pulsed faintly, phosphorescent against the grime.

"This is the place." Alex killed the flashlight, letting her eyes adjust. "Tell me you brought the iron filings."
```

**Recommendations**:
1. Apply similar sensory density throughout the scene
2. Maintain the mundane/magical contrast ratio established here
3. Continue grounding Alex's competence in physical preparation
4. Build atmosphere through accumulating specific details

**PASS TO**: Lead Agent (for scene integration) or back to Aria (if this triggers plot questions about the ritual's nature)
