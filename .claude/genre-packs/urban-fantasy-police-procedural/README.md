## Workflow Recommendations

### Recommended Series Planning Flow

1. **Phase 1: Series Foundation**
   - Select "Urban Fantasy Police Procedural" as genre

   - Complete `urban-fantasy-world-template.md`
   - Establish magic system with clear rules and limitations
   - Define supernatural society structure
   - Create investigation framework (how magic affects police work)

3. **Phase 3: Character Planning**
   - Complete `detective-character-template.md` for protagonist
   - Create supporting cast (partner, captain, informants, love interest)
   - Plan character arcs across series

4. **Phase 4: Relationship Progressions**
   - Apply `relationship-progression.md` patterns
   - Plan partnership evolution book-by-book
   - Plan romantic arc (if applicable) with appropriate heat level

5. **Phase 5: Plot Threads & Series Arcs**
   - Use `series-arc-integration.md` for multi-book planning

   - Plan series-long conspiracy/mystery

6. **Phase 6: Series Bible**
   - Compile all planning into comprehensive bible
   - Validate with genre-specific checklists

   - Understand book's position in series

   - Apply 3-Act or 5-Act structure as base

   - Weave series arc through case investigation

   - Track character growth through investigation
   - Plan relationship progression beats

7. **Phase 7: Validation**
   - Run all validation checklists
   - Verify fair-play mystery structure
   - Confirm procedural authenticity

## Genre Pack Principles

### Core Genre Elements

**Urban Fantasy Police Procedural requires:**

1. **Grounded Urban Setting**
   - Real or believable fictional city
   - Contemporary timeframe
   - Mundane world continues alongside magical

2. **Procedural Authenticity**
   - Realistic investigation techniques
   - Police protocols (adapted for supernatural)
   - Departmental politics and bureaucracy
   - Legal framework and jurisdictional issues

3. **Magical World-Building**
   - Consistent magic system with rules and costs
   - Supernatural species/factions with politics
   - Integration of magic into investigation work
   - Masquerade or public magic (defined clearly)

4. **Investigation-Driven Plot**
   - Case structure with discovery ‚Üí resolution arc
   - Fair-play mystery elements
   - Clues and evidence progression
   - Logical deduction and detective work

5. **Character-Focused Development**
   - Protagonist with detective skills AND character arc
   - Relationships (partnership, romance, found family)
   - Personal stakes in professional cases
   - Growth through investigation challenges

### Critical Balance Points

**Mundane vs. Magical:**
- Magic should enhance, not replace, detective work
- Best moments: supernatural intrudes on ordinary routine
- Procedural grounding prevents "magic solves everything"

**Standalone vs. Serial:**
- Each book has complete case resolution
- Series arc advances without overshadowing case
- New readers can enter series at any book
- Returning readers get payoff for continuity

**Mystery vs. Thriller:**
- Fair-play mystery: Reader can solve alongside detective
- Character knowledge tracking prevents violations
- Clues planted before revelations
- Dramatic irony used intentionally

**Professional vs. Personal:**
- Cases reflect character themes and arcs
- Relationships develop through shared investigations
- Work-life balance creates realistic tension
- Job affects personal life and vice versa

## Common Pitfalls (And How to Avoid Them)

### ‚ùå Magic as Deus Ex Machina

**Problem:** New magical ability appears exactly when needed to solve case

**Solution:**
- Establish all magical abilities in series planning
- Magic has costs and limitations
- Magical solutions require setup and foreshadowing
- Detective work solves case, not convenient spell

**Reference:** `validation-rules/magic-system-consistency.md`

---

### ‚ùå Character Knowledge Violations

**Problem:** Detective acts on information they haven't discovered yet

**Solution:**
- Track character knowledge state before every scene
- Log all knowledge gains during writing
- Validate actions match available information
- Use MCP character-planning-server for tracking

**Reference:** `validation-rules/character-knowledge-tracking.md`

---

### ‚ùå Investigation Logic Failures

**Problem:** Case is solved through coincidence or impossible deductions

**Solution:**
- Plan evidence discovery timing during book planning
- Ensure clues are planted before breakthroughs
- Validate deduction logic (what does character know?)
- Use fair-play mystery structure

**Reference:** `validation-rules/investigation-logic.md`

---

### ‚ùå Procedural Inauthenticity

**Problem:** Police work feels unrealistic or rules are ignored without consequence

**Solution:**
- Research basic police procedures
- Adapt procedures logically for supernatural context
- Show consequences when rules are broken
- Include bureaucracy and politics
- Consult detective-logan agent for validation

**Reference:** `style-guides/procedural-authenticity.md`

---

### ‚ùå Info-Dump World-Building

**Problem:** Magic system explained through exposition rather than shown through action

**Solution:**
- Reveal world rules through investigation work
- Use dialogue only for character-known information
- Magical forensics demonstrates magic system
- Supernatural creatures behaving naturally shows society

**Reference:** `style-guides/worldbuilding-integration.md`

---

### ‚ùå Arc Stalling

**Problem:** Series arc doesn't advance; each book repeats same information

**Solution:**
- Each book must answer at least one series question
- Each book must raise at least one new series question
- Arc antagonist demonstrates new capability
- Protagonist's understanding of arc deepens

**Reference:** `beat-sheets/series-arc-integration.md`
