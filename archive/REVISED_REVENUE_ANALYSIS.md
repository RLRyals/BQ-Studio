## PART 4: PRINT VS EBOOK REVENUE

### Print-on-Demand Economics

**Royalty Structure:**
- **60% royalty:** Books priced ≥$9.99
- **50% royalty:** Books priced <$9.99 (effective June 10, 2025)
- **40% royalty:** Expanded distribution

**Printing Costs (Example: 300-page paperback, 6x9, B&W):**
- Printing cost: ~$4.50
- List price: $14.99
- Royalty: (60% × $14.99) - $4.50 = **$4.49 per sale**

**Typical Print vs Ebook Split:**
- **Ebook:** 70-85% of sales
- **Print:** 15-30% of sales
- **Audiobook:** 10-20% additional (if produced)

**5-Book Series Print Revenue Example:**

Assuming:
- 100 ebook sales/month across series
- 20 print sales/month (20% of ebook volume)
- $4.49 royalty per print sale

**Monthly Print Revenue:** 20 × $4.49 = **$90/month**

**This is supplemental income, not primary.**
