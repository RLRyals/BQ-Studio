## SERIES PREMISE

**Elevator Pitch:**
In 1885 London, the legendary Sweetwater Confectionery Factory has been closed for ten years‚Äîever since master inventor Cornelius Sweetwater's daughter vanished and the killings began. When investigative journalist Theodore Grey receives an invitation to tour the factory, he discovers the truth: the factory is a gilded prison, and the Beautiful Monster locked inside is both more dangerous and more tragic than anyone imagined.

**High Concept:**
What if Willy Wonka's factory was built to imprison a serial killer daughter? Gothic horror meets steampunk whimsy in a deadly wonderland where candy kills, clockwork hearts tick with human blood, and the greatest invention is the cage itself.

## OVERARCHING SERIES ARC

### The Central Mystery
**Question:** Why does Vivienne Sweetwater kill, and can she be saved‚Äîor must she be destroyed?

**The Truth (Revealed Across 5 Books):**
Cornelius Sweetwater was a genius inventor obsessed with immortality. When his wife died in childbirth, he experimented on their newborn daughter Vivienne, replacing her failing heart with a clockwork mechanism powered by aetheric energy. The procedure saved her life but altered her fundamentally: every seven days, her mechanical heart requires "winding"‚Äînot with a key, but with the death of another human. Their life force feeds the mechanism. Without it, she dies.

Horrified but unable to reverse the procedure, Cornelius built the factory as a wonderland prison: beautiful enough to make her captivity bearable, secure enough to protect the world from her, and filled with his desperate attempts to find a cure. For twenty-five years, he's "fed" her heart with carefully selected victims‚Äîcriminals, the dying, those society wouldn't miss. But Vivienne knows what she is. She's tired of killing. She wants to die.

The factory itself is Cornelius's greatest and most terrible invention: a sentient, mechanical wonderland that evolves, adapts, and protects its prisoner‚Äîwhether she wants protection or not.

### Series-Level Conflict
- **External:** Theodore must expose the truth about Sweetwater Factory while society (and powerful figures invested in keeping it secret) tries to stop him
- **Internal:** Can Theodore save Vivienne without becoming complicit in murder? Can love exist between someone who must kill to live and someone dedicated to truth?
- **Thematic:** What is the cost of genius? Is immortality (even mechanical) worth the price in human lives? Who gets to decide if a monster deserves mercy?

## FACTORY AS CHARACTER

The Sweetwater Confectionery Factory is a **living, sentient, mechanical wonderland** that evolves across the series.

**Book 1:** Whimsical but Wrong (candy-colored horror, sweet smells hiding decay)
**Book 2:** Protective Prison (factory defends Vivienne, attacks intruders)
**Book 3:** Active Threat (factory begins hunting, killing on its own)
**Book 4:** Weapon (Cornelius weaponizes factory, it becomes extension of his will)
**Book 5:** Dying Beast (as factory is destroyed, it fights back desperately)

**Factory Rooms (One Focus Per Book):**
1. **The Greeting Hall** - Where visitors enter, never leave (Book 1)

## VICTORIAN GOTHIC STEAMPUNK ELEMENTS

**Victorian Society Constraints:**
- Theodore's reputation ruined if he's linked to factory
- Vivienne could never exist in society (woman + killer + mechanical = triple scandal)
- Class divide: victims are poor, perpetrators are rich
- Gender: Vivienne's existence violates every Victorian rule for women

**Steampunk Technology:**
- Clockwork heart (brass, gears, aetheric power)
- Factory automation (mechanical servants, conveyor systems)
- Steam-powered security (pressure locks, scalding traps)
- Aetheric energy (Victorian magical science)
- Mechanical augmentation (Victorian transhumanism)

**Gothic Atmosphere:**
- Factory is isolated (London edge, fog-shrouded)
- Decay meets opulence (gilded cages, rotting confections)
- Body horror (mechanical parts, candy made with human ingredients)
- Psychological horror (Vivienne's guilt, Theodore's complicity)
- Trapped forever (factory won't release them)
