### Phase 2: Worldbuilding Bible (COMPLETE)
**Lead**: Professor Mira (Worldbuilding & Magic Systems)
**Document**: `worldbuilding-bible.md`

**Established Systems**:
- **Vampirism**: Molecular disease (Vital Resonance Theory), not supernatural curse
- **Blood Requirement**: 1 pint every 72 hours minimum, 3 pints every 48 hours optimal
- **Sunlight Vulnerability**: UV radiation destabilizes corrupted cells, death in 60 seconds direct sun
- **The Gardens**: 3 levels underground (12,000 sq ft total), 40 feet beneath 47 Cheyne Walk, Chelsea
- **Power System**: 200 lbs coal/day, steam-powered, can't run unsupervised >2 months
- **Cure Theory**: Counter-resonance frequency + alchemical catalyst, success rate starts at 0.3%
- **Deadman's Switch**: 3-phase destruction system, triggered if Celeste dies or Gardens breached
- **Timeline**: Series begins 1885, 3 years before Jack the Ripper (1888)
- **Vampire Society**: ~50 vampires in London, Celeste is outcast, Court of Shadows in Westminster

**Technology Level**:
- Celeste operates at 1920s-1930s equivalent in specific fields
- 40 years ahead in mechanical computation
- 30 years ahead in precision engineering
- NO electricity (pre-Edison in London)
- Steam/pneumatic/mechanical only

**Critical Constraints**:
- 72-hour blood requirement (non-negotiable)
- Maintenance requirement (Augustus essential)
- Coal consumption (200 lbs/day minimum)
- Victorian social constraints (gender, class)
- No electricity rule (absolute)

## üìä SERIES OVERVIEW

### Proposed 5-Book Structure

**Book 1: "The Eternal Garden"** (Foundations)
- Margot breaks into Gardens, hired by Augustus as keeper
- Celeste's backstory revealed (murders, vampire transformation)
- First cure attempt = spectacular failure
- Establish Celeste-Margot dynamic

**Book 2: "The Clockwork Cure"** (Complications)
- Second cure attempt = partial success with terrible side effects
- Level 3 of Gardens discovered
- External threat: Margot's past catches up
- Cure research reveals vampirism origin theories

**Book 3: "The Mechanical Heart"** (Midpoint Crisis)
- Third cure attempt = Split Celeste emerges (temporary humanity)
- Human-Celeste begs Margot to destroy research
- Vampire-Celeste dismisses human-self as "chemical disturbance"
- Augustus dies (removes moral compass)

**Book 4: "The Brass Requiem"** (Escalation)
- Fourth cure attempt = success possible but requires sacrifice
- Margot now sole keeper, must decide if she stays
- External threats converge (hunters, Court, authorities)
- Deadman's Switch nearly triggered

**Book 5: "The Last Invention"** (Resolution)
- Final cure attempt (success? transformation? tragedy?)
- Does Celeste deserve cure if achieved?
- Margot's choice: forgiveness, departure, or something else?
- Wonderland's fate resolved
