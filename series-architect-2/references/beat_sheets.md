# Beat Sheet Framework Guidance

Comprehensive guide to selecting and applying beat sheet frameworks from the beat_sheet_library.

---

## Purpose

Beat sheets provide the structural skeleton for your story, mapping major plot and emotional turning points across your manuscript. This guide helps you select the most appropriate framework for your project.

---

## Available Beat Sheet Library

### Core Structural Frameworks

#### 1. Three-Act Structure (`three_act_structure.md`)
**Best For:** All genres, traditional narrative arcs
**Structure:** 25% Act 1 / 50% Act 2 / 25% Act 3
**Key Beats:** 8-12 major turning points
**Strengths:**
- Universally recognized structure
- Works across all genres
- Clear beginning/middle/end delineation
- Easy to teach and understand

**Use When:**
- Writing in any genre outside romance
- Need simple, proven framework
- Working with traditional publishing expectations
- Teaching story structure to collaborators

## Selection Decision Tree

### Step 1: Identify Primary Genre

**Romance-focused (romance is the plot):**
- Contemporary, low fantasy ‚Üí Romance Beat Sheet
- Paranormal, fantasy world ‚Üí Romantasy 20 Beats or Romantasy - The Beats
- Dark themes, morally gray ‚Üí Dark Romancing The Beats

**Romance + substantial external plot:**
- Equal weight romance/adventure ‚Üí Romantasy - The Beats
- Fantasy quest with romance subplot ‚Üí Romantasy 20 Beats

**Non-romance:**
- Any other genre ‚Üí Three-Act Structure

### Step 2: Check for Trauma Themes

**If trauma/recovery is central to character arc:**
- Both MCs trauma survivors ‚Üí A: Mutual Trauma Bonding
- Protector/wounded dynamic ‚Üí B: Protector √ó Wounded
- Addiction/destructive patterns ‚Üí C: Toxic Coping ‚Üí Healing
- PTSD/reintegration ‚Üí D: Post-Trauma Re-Entry
- Dark romance + trauma ‚Üí E: Dark Trauma Hybrid

### Step 3: Validate Heat Level Compatibility

Match your target heat level to framework range:
- Heat 1-2 (clean/sweet) ‚Üí Romance Beat Sheet, D: Post-Trauma Re-Entry
- Heat 2-3 (moderate) ‚Üí Romance Beat Sheet, Romantasy 20 Beats, A-D frameworks
- Heat 3-4 (steamy) ‚Üí Romantasy - The Beats, Dark Romancing, A-E frameworks
- Heat 5 (explicit) ‚Üí Romantasy - The Beats, Dark Romancing, E: Dark Trauma Hybrid

### Step 4: Consider Series vs. Standalone

**Series:**
- Use same framework across all books for consistency
- Romantasy 20 Beats designed for series
- Traumancing frameworks can extend across series

**Standalone:**
- Any framework works
- Three-Act Structure simplest for one-off projects

## Using a Beat Sheet

### 1. Load the Selected Framework
Read the entire beat sheet to understand:
- Overall structure and phase progression
- Target word count percentages for each beat
- Required story elements at each turning point
- Special features (consent checkpoints, worldbuilding beats, etc.)

### 2. Map Your Story to Beats
For each beat in the framework:
- **Scene description:** What literally happens
- **Character development:** How MCs change
- **Emotional beats:** What readers should feel
- **Plot advancement:** How external story progresses
- **Relationship progression:** (Romance) Where are MCs emotionally?

### 3. Customize as Needed
Beat sheets are guides, not prison bars:
- **Add beats** for complex subplots
- **Merge beats** if story is simpler
- **Adjust percentages** slightly for pacing
- **Skip optional beats** if not relevant

**NEVER skip:**
- Opening image / setup
- Inciting incident / meet-cute
- Midpoint / turning point
- Climax / black moment
- Resolution / HEA/HFN

### 4. Integrate with Other Planning
Beat sheets work alongside:
- **Character arcs:** Map character transformation to beat progression
- **Worldbuilding:** Ensure world rules create obstacles at key beats
- **Subplot tracking:** Weave B/C plots through main beat structure
- **Trope execution:** Deliver trope payoffs at emotionally optimal beats

## Framework Combinations

### For Complex Projects

**Multi-POV Romance:**
- Primary POV: Full beat sheet
- Secondary POV: Mirror beats from their perspective
- Ensure beats hit from both angles

**Romance + Mystery/Thriller:**
- Romance beats: Relationship progression
- Three-Act Structure: Mystery/thriller plot
- Integrate both so they support each other

**Series with Overarching Plot:**
- Book-level: Romance beat sheet per book
- Series-level: Three-act structure across all books
- Each book = one act of overall series arc

## Customizing Beat Sheets

### When to Create Custom Framework

Create new beat sheet when:
- Existing frameworks don't fit your genre
- Unique structure required (non-linear, epistolary, etc.)
- Blending multiple genres in unusual ways
- Series has consistent unique structure

**How to create:** See `references/create_new_beat_frameworks.md`

### Common Modifications

**Extend Romance Beats:**
- Add beats for polyamory (multiple relationship arcs)
- Add beats for LGBTQ+ specific elements (coming out, chosen family)
- Add beats for disability representation (access, accommodation, identity)

**Compress for Novellas:**
- Merge adjacent beats
- Reduce from 12-20 beats to 8-10 beats
- Maintain opening, midpoint, climax, resolution

**Expand for Epic:**
- Split each act into sub-acts
- Add worldbuilding/politics beats
- Extend from 20 beats to 30+ for door-stoppers

## Troubleshooting

### "None of these fit my story"
**Solution:** Use Three-Act Structure as base, customize heavily, or create custom framework (see create_new_beat_frameworks.md)

### "I need elements from two frameworks"
**Solution:** Choose primary framework for structure, cherry-pick specific beats from secondary as needed

### "Beat sheet feels formulaic"
**Solution:** Beat sheets provide structure, not content. Unique voice, characters, and world make story distinctive. Structure enables creativity, doesn't constrain it.

### "My story doesn't fit percentages"
**Solution:** Percentages are guidelines. ¬±5% variation is normal. Ensure major beats hit in correct order, exact word count less important.
