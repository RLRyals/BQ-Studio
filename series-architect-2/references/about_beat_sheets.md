# Beat Sheet Frameworks

Reference guide for structural frameworks. Load when user needs to select or customize a beat sheet structure.

---

## Framework Selection Guide

**Choose based on:**
- Genre conventions (romance → relationship beats, thriller → revelation beats)
- User familiarity (use frameworks they know)
- Story needs (character-driven → Truby, plot-driven → Save the Cat)
- Flexibility preference (rigid → 22-step, flexible → 3-act)

---

## 1. Three-Act Structure (Classic)

**Best for:** Flexible approach, any genre, discovery writers

**Beats:**

### Act 1 (25%)
1. **Opening Image** - World before change
2. **Inciting Incident** - Disruption
3. **First Plot Point** - Point of no return, enters Act 2

### Act 2 (50%)
4. **Rising Action** - Complications increase
5. **Midpoint** - False victory/defeat, stakes raise
6. **Complications** - Pressure increases
7. **All Is Lost** - Lowest point
8. **Second Plot Point** - Final push, enters Act 3

### Act 3 (25%)
9. **Climax** - Final confrontation
10. **Resolution** - New equilibrium
11. **Closing Image** - World after change

**Variations:**
- Add pinch points between acts for pacing
- Expand Act 2 into 2A (fun and games) and 2B (bad guys close in)

## 2. Save the Cat (Blake Snyder)

**Best for:** Commercial fiction, screenwriting-influenced structure

**15 Beats:**

1. **Opening Image** (0-1%)
2. **Theme Stated** (5%)
3. **Setup** (1-10%)
4. **Catalyst** (10%)
5. **Debate** (10-20%)
6. **Break into Two** (20%)
7. **B Story** (22%)
8. **Fun and Games** (20-50%)
9. **Midpoint** (50%)
10. **Bad Guys Close In** (50-75%)
11. **All Is Lost** (75%)
12. **Dark Night of the Soul** (75-80%)
13. **Break into Three** (80%)
14. **Finale** (80-99%)
15. **Final Image** (99-100%)

**Percentages** indicate approximate placement in manuscript.

## 3. Hero's Journey (Joseph Campbell)

**Best for:** Fantasy, adventure, transformation stories

**12 Stages:**

### Act 1: Departure
1. **Ordinary World**
2. **Call to Adventure**
3. **Refusal of the Call**
4. **Meeting the Mentor**
5. **Crossing the Threshold**

### Act 2: Initiation
6. **Tests, Allies, Enemies**
7. **Approach to the Inmost Cave**
8. **Ordeal**
9. **Reward (Seizing the Sword)**

### Act 3: Return
10. **The Road Back**
11. **Resurrection**
12. **Return with the Elixir**

## 4. Truby 22-Step (Flexible Adaptation)

**Best for:** Character-driven, moral complexity, literary fiction

**Note:** Use flexibly - not all steps required, order can vary

### Setup
1. **Self-Revelation, Need, and Desire** - Where character will end up
2. **Ghost and Story World** - Wound and environment
3. **Weakness and Need** - Flaw holding them back
4. **Inciting Event** - Life disrupted
5. **Desire** - What they want (may be wrong goal)
6. **Ally or Allies** - Support system
7. **Opponent and/or Mystery** - What stands in the way
8. **Fake-Ally Opponent** - Betrayal source
9. **First Revelation and Decision** - New information, choice

### Building
10. **Plan** - Strategy to achieve desire
11. **Opponent's Plan and Main Counterattack** - Opposition strategy
12. **Drive** - Executing plan, complications
13. **Attack by Ally** - Support challenged

### Climax
14. **Apparent Defeat** - Seems lost
15. **Second Revelation and Decision** - Deeper truth
16. **Audience Revelation** - Reader understands something character doesn't
17. **Third Revelation and Decision** - Character understands
18. **Gate, Gauntlet, Visit to Death** - Final test

### Resolution
19. **Battle** - Final confrontation (internal/external)
20. **Self-Revelation** - Character sees truth about themselves
21. **Moral Decision** - Choice reflecting growth
22. **New Equilibrium** - Changed world

**Adaptation Notes:**
- Can compress to 12-15 essential beats
- Revelations can be combined
- Opponent can be internal (addiction, fear) rather than person
- Moral dimension optional based on story type

## 5. Romance Beat Sheet

**Best for:** Romance genre (any subgenre)

**12 Beats:**

1. **Meet-Cute** - First encounter (memorable, revealing)
2. **No Way!** - Reasons they can't be together established
3. **Attraction Building** - Chemistry develops
4. **First Kiss** - Physical intimacy begins
5. **Commitment Issues Surface** - Internal/external obstacles
6. **Deepening Relationship** - Emotional intimacy
7. **Raising Stakes** - External plot pressure (if applicable)
8. **Everything's Great** - False security
9. **Black Moment** - Relationship seems doomed
10. **Grand Gesture** - One proves devotion
11. **Confession** - Emotional vulnerability, truth revealed
12. **HEA/HFN** - Happily Ever After or For Now

**Additional Tracking:**
- Heat level progression (when do intimate scenes occur)
- Emotional arc beats
- External plot integration points

---

## 6. Mystery/Thriller Beat Sheet

**Best for:** Mystery, thriller, suspense

**14 Beats:**

1. **Normal World**
2. **Crime/Inciting Incident**
3. **Investigation Begins**
4. **First Clue**
5. **False Lead**
6. **Second Clue**
7. **Midpoint Revelation** - New understanding of crime
8. **Complications**
9. **Third Clue** - Critical piece
10. **Red Herring Exposed**
11. **All Is Lost** - Investigation seems failed
12. **Final Clue** - Breakthrough
13. **Confrontation** - Reveal antagonist
14. **Resolution** - Justice/new equilibrium

**Clue Placement Strategy:**
- Plant 3-5 real clues
- Plant 2-3 red herrings
- Each clue scene advances investigation
- Final clue should be present from early (hidden in plain sight)

## 7. Custom Beat Sheet Creation

Build custom framework by combining elements:

**Core Framework** (choose one):
- 3-act
- 4-act
- 5-act
- Hero's journey

**Add Genre-Specific Beats:**
- Romance beats (if applicable)
- Mystery reveals (if applicable)
- Horror scares (if applicable)
- Thriller tension peaks

**Character Arc Integration:**
- Wound established (early)
- Misbelief challenged (middle)
- Transformation moment (climax)
- New truth embraced (resolution)

**Example Custom: Romantic Thriller**
- Base: 3-act structure
- Add: Romance beats (meet-cute, first kiss, black moment, confession)
- Add: Mystery beats (clues, red herrings, revelation)
- Integrate: Character wounds tied to both romance and mystery

## Using Beat Sheets in Series Architect

**Single Book:**
1. Select framework (user choice or AI suggestion)
2. Adapt to genre needs
3. Fill in beats with story-specific content
4. Expand beats into chapter summaries

**Multi-Book Series:**
1. Select framework for each book
2. Can use same framework across series or vary
3. Ensure series-level beats (overarching conflict milestones) align
4. Track escalation across books

**Flexibility:**
- Beats are guideposts, not rigid structure
- Can skip beats if not relevant
- Can add beats if story needs
- Order can shift for specific story needs
