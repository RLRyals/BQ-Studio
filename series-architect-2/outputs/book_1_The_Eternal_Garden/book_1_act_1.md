# Book 1 Act 1: The Cage
## Chapters 1-5 (~18,000 words)

**Act Goal**: Establish world, characters, and central conflict
**Emotional Arc**: Margot from confident thief → terrified captive → horrified witness
**Celeste Arc**: Isolated inventor → curious about visitor → reveals true monstrous nature
**Act Turn**: Margot learns full horror of Celeste's backstory (7 pre-vampire murders)

---

## Chapter 1: Margot's POV - "The Heist" (~3,500 words)

### Scene Summary
Margot breaks into 47 Cheyne Walk and discovers the underground Hartwell Mechanical Gardens.

### Setting
Victorian London streets, foggy night, 1885

### Major Story Events (6-8 events)
1. Margot cases 47 Cheyne Walk (wealthy inventor's house, father recently died)
2. Breaks in through coal chute (traditional thief methods)
3. Discovers entrance to underground levels (unexpected)
4. Descends to Gardens Level 1 (mechanical marvels beyond imagination)
5. Explores in awe and greed (this is the score of a lifetime)
6. First wonder: Mechanical gardens, pneumatic systems, gas-lit beauty
7. Movement in shadows - realizes she's not alone
8. **Cliffhanger**: Celeste reveals presence

### Character Introduction: Margot
- Competent thief, mid-late 20s
- Intelligent, observant, confident
- Running from something (hinted, not explained)
- Working-class background, street-smart
- Shows: competence, boldness, greed, wonder

### Worldbuilding Reveals
- Victorian London atmosphere (fog, gas lamps, cobblestones)
- 47 Cheyne Walk (wealthy Chelsea address)
- Coal chute entrance (period-accurate entry point)
- Gardens Level 1 layout (12,000 sq ft, mechanical wonders)
- Steampunk aesthetic established (gears, steam, pneumatics)

### Emotional Beats
- **Opening**: Confidence, calculated risk
- **Discovery**: Awe at mechanical marvels
- **Greed**: This is the score of a lifetime
- **Fear**: Not alone, trapped?

### Pacing Notes
- Hook: Open with Margot casing the building (immediate action)
- Build: Slow reveal of Gardens (sensory wonder)
- Turn: Movement in shadows (danger)

## Chapter 2: Celeste's POV - "The Visitor" (~3,500 words)

### Scene Summary
Celeste reveals herself to Margot, cordial and unsettling. Margot realizes all exits are sealed.

### Setting
Gardens workshop, evening (Celeste aware of intruder for 20 minutes)

### Major Story Events (6-8 events)
1. Celeste observing Margot through pneumatic cameras (voyeuristic delight)
2. Internal monologue: First interesting person in 15 years
3. Decision: Kill her or keep her? (intellectual puzzle)
4. Celeste reveals herself cordially (no aggression, just curiosity)
5. Witty, intellectual conversation (Celeste treating this as game)
6. Margot attempts exits - all sealed automatically
7. Margot's confidence → fear as situation dawns
8. **Chapter End**: "You're the first person to reach Level One. Would you like to see Level Two?"

### Character Introduction: Celeste
- Late 20s appearance (vampire, actually ~18 years transformed)
- Brilliant inventor, clinical detachment
- Genuinely delighted by visitor (not threat, novelty)
- Witty, charming, unsettling
- Shows: intelligence, curiosity, lack of normal fear response

### Worldbuilding Reveals
- Pneumatic camera system (surveillance)
- Automated security (exits seal when intruder detected)
- Celeste lives alone underground (15 years)
- Gardens have multiple levels (Level 2 mentioned)

### Emotional Beats
- **Celeste**: Delight, curiosity, puzzle-solving pleasure
- **Margot**: Confidence → confusion → fear → trapped realization

### Pacing Notes
- Tension: Celeste's calm vs. Margot's growing panic
- Dialogue: Witty, quick, reveals character
- Turn: "You're trapped" realization

## Chapter 4: Celeste's POV - "The Truth" (~3,500 words)

### Scene Summary
Margot confronts Celeste about the journal. Celeste matter-of-factly reveals she's a vampire.

### Setting
Gardens laboratory, evening of Day 2

### Major Story Events (6-8 events)
1. Margot confronts Celeste: "What are you?"
2. Celeste reveals vampirism (matter-of-fact, clinical)
3. Backstory begins: Attacked 1883, turned, locked herself away
4. Blood supply explanation (automated filtration, stored reserves shown)
5. Margot's horror: Trapped with vampire
6. Celeste's logic: "I haven't killed in fifteen years. You're safer here than in London."
7. Celeste's proposition: "You're going to help me perfect the cure."
8. **Chapter End**: Celeste explains she NEEDS Margot's help

### Vampire Biology Established
- Vampirism as curse/disease
- Blood requirement (not specified yet, but necessary)
- Sunlight fatal (mentioned)
- Strength, speed enhanced
- Hasn't killed in 15 years (automated blood supply)

### Worldbuilding Reveals
- Celeste transformed 1883 (15 years vampire)
- Self-imposed imprisonment (built Gardens as cage)
- Cure research (mechanical solution to biological problem)
- Blood supply automation (prevents killing)

### Emotional Beats
- **Margot**: Terror, trapped realization, survival mode
- **Celeste**: Genuine confusion at Margot's fear (lacks empathy)

### Pacing Notes
- Revelation: Build dread, then confirm (vampire)

## Chapter 5: Flashback/Margot POV - "The Murders" (~4,000 words)

### Scene Summary
Celeste narrates her backstory: 7 pre-vampire murders in Whitechapel, Augustus's discovery, vampire attack, transformation.

### Setting
Gardens library, Day 3 (Celeste narrates, Margot listens)

### Major Story Events (8-10 events)
1. **1882 Whitechapel**: Celeste pre-vampire, age 18, already a killer
2. **The Dissections**: 7 victims (street urchins), surgical precision, obsessive need to understand
3. **Motivation**: Not malice - obsessive curiosity about how things work (including people)
4. **Augustus's Discovery**: Father finds Celeste in workshop with Subject 7 (street urchin mid-dissection)
5. **The Attack**: Same night, vampire in fog attacks Celeste
6. **The Transformation**: Bite, blood exchange, death, resurrection
7. **Immediate Aftermath**: Celeste kills attacking vampire, kills 3 Court enforcers who arrive
8. **Augustus's Choice**: Help daughter or turn her in (chose help)
9. **Gardens Purpose**: Built as cage, not just workshop (2-year construction, 1883-1885)
10. **Key Line**: "The vampire didn't make me a monster. It gave the monster eternity."

### Celeste's Psychology Revealed
- **Primary psychopath**: Lack of empathy is innate, not learned
- **Intellectual curiosity**: Obsessive need to understand how things work
- **No remorse**: Genuinely doesn't understand why murders are wrong
- **Self-aware**: Knows she's dangerous, chose imprisonment

### Margot's Reaction
- Horror at pre-vampire murders (curse didn't create monster)
- Realization: Celeste chose cage (some awareness of wrongness)
- Terror: Trapped with someone who dissected 7 people
- Question: Is cure possible for someone who never had humanity?

### Emotional Beats
- **Celeste**: Clinical retelling, no emotion, confusion at Margot's horror
- **Margot**: Progressive horror, nausea, trapped with serial killer

### Pacing Notes
- Slow reveal: Build from "I had a problem" to full horror
- Clinical tone: Celeste's detachment increases horror
- Turn: "Vampire didn't make me a monster"
