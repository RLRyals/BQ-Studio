**Genre**: Steampunk Gothic Horror

**Timeline**: 1886, approximately 4 months (3-month time jump from Book 1, then 4 months)

---

## Book Premise

Three months after the disastrous first cure attempt, Margot has settled into her role as Celeste's keeper and research partner. But when Margot's past arrives in London seeking her, and the Vampire Court demands proof of progress, both women face new pressures. The second cure attempt succeeds—briefly—giving Celeste emotions she finds "inconvenient" before a violent reversion doubles her hunger. As external threats intensify and Level 3 of the Gardens reveals Augustus's darkest secrets, Margot must confront a terrifying question: Is she helping Celeste become human, or is Celeste helping her become something else?

## Cure Attempt #2 (Midpoint)

**Theory**: Counter-resonance frequency + alchemical blood catalyst

**Method**:
- Mechanical vibration apparatus (rebuilt from Book 1)
- Biological component: Blood-bonded elixir (vampire blood + human blood + rare herbs)
- 24-hour sustained treatment

**Result**: PARTIAL SUCCESS WITH TERRIBLE SIDE EFFECTS
- Success phase (8 hours): Celeste experiences emotions
  - Fear, empathy, horror at own actions
  - Begs Margot: "If this doesn't last, don't let me continue research"
  - Humanity is suffering, not liberation
- Reversion (hour 9): Violent, catastrophic
  - Vampire traits return amplified
  - Blood requirement doubles (2 pints every 72 hours)
  - Hunger more intense than baseline
- Success Rate: 5% (improved from 0.3%)

**Learning**: Biological component necessary, but creates dependency/rebound effect
